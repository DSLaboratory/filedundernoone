For eleven years, Margaret Vane sat at the Registry and decided what the country was allowed to see. She was good at it. Then she opened a ledger she was meant to file and not read — and found it was quietly killing the living by writing them down as dead. She did the correct thing. She reported it. By morning she had never existed.

Margaret Vane spent eleven years at the Registry deciding what the country was allowed to see — the watcher who chose the blind spots, posted the guards, aimed the lights. When she finds a ledger that files the living as dead and reports it, she is struck from every register that ever held her. For eleven weeks she is no one. Then she goes back in — past her own guards, through her own blind spots — to take back the proof that she was real.
